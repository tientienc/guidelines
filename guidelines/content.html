Writing Goals and Principles

With every word we write, we aim to:

Educate

  • Help our audience understand what we are offering and how they can benefit from it.
  • Guide them through our processes step by step without being patronizing.

Motivate

  • Encourage users to take control of their health and improve their lifestyle.
  • Show customers and other researchers why it is a great idea to partner with us.
  • Get the general public excited about taking part in our research studies and pilots.

Build relationships

  • Give our audience the chance to get to know LifeQ, and give them opportunities to tell us more about themselves.
  • This will go a long way toward earning their trust and loyalty.

Inspire

  • Get our audience excited about our offering by being enthusiastic and confident.
  • Make users, customers and the general public want to join our journey toward optimal health for all.

Delight

  • Create content that will put smiles on people’s faces and leave them wanting more.

To achieve these goals, our writing must be:

Relevant

  • Tell our audience exactly what they need to know (no more, no less), and show how it pertains to them.
  • “Tell readers what they need to know, not just what we want to say. Give them the exact information they need, along with opportunities to learn more. Remember that you’re the expert, and readers don’t have access to everything you know.” (MailChimp Style Guide)

Clear

  • “Understand the topic you’re writing about. Use simple words and sentences.” (MailChimp Style Guide)
  • “Write for a Grade 7 reading level.” (Shopify Style Guide)
  • Avoid jargon.

Truthful

  • Write accurately and honestly - we are not selling a product; we are building relationships.

Engaging

  • Be interesting and witty (where appropriate).
  • Write in a way that makes our audience want to read.

Friendly

  • “Write like a human. Don’t be afraid to break a few rules if it makes your writing more relatable.” (MailChimp Style Guide)

Tone: Whom are we speaking to?

While our voice remains constant, we adapt our tone according to whom we are speaking to, what we are talking about, and what our audience is feeling.

Here are the basic guidelines:

User-facing

Credible, informative, friendly, human + helpful, trustworthy, encouraging, enthusiastic, witty

E.g.: LifeQ Explorer app, LifeQ User Portal, study recruitment touchpoints, social media posts

B2B

Credible, informative, friendly, human + collaborative, confident (in our product), and enthusiastic (about how our customers can benefit from our product)

E.g.: client presentations, business emails, etc.

B2R

Credible, informative, friendly, human + collaborative

E.g.: scientific documentation like white papers

Internal

Credible, informative, friendly, human + fun, caring, enthusiastic, encouraging

Consistency

We strive for consistency in everything we write.

We must consistently use the same nouns and verbs: “Each important object and action should have a single word to represent it.” (Shopify Style Guide)

To ensure consistency in all our user-facing written content in terms of terminology, grammar, punctuation, voice, tone, etc., we have the Copy Style Guide.
