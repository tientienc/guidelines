Copy Style Guide

Tone

  1. Credible, informative, friendly, witty and to the point.
  2. When it comes to wit, subtlety and restraint are key.

Target Audience

  1. Health enthusiasts and non-health enthusiasts in our group of users - ranging from those who are very educated regarding their health, body metrics and fitness, to those who know nothing. We aim for the

Purpose of UX Copy

  1. To inform and guide the user.
  2. To make instructions easy to follow and actions easy to complete.
  3. To provide a delightful user experience.

Length

  1. Apps, email campaigns, social media posts and recruitment elements: Keep the copy as short, clear and to the point as possible.
  2. The Asset pages and Help Site are longer and more comprehensive. Clarity takes precedence over brevity.

US English

Always use US English. Here are some examples of what to look out for:

  1. Use “o” spelling instead of “ou” spelling, e.g. color/favor NOT colour/favour.
  2. Use “-ize” endings instead of “-ise”, e.g. organize NOT organise.
  3. Look out for certain preferred word choices, e.g. last name NOT surname.

Time and dates

  1. As far as possible, dates must be written in US English format:
    • Month Day, Year (e.g. September 26, 2017)
    • Comma between day of week and date, e.g. Wednesday, September 21
  2. Avoid all-numeral dates, e.g. 09/26/2017.
  3. In dates, use cardinal numbers (1, 2, 3, etc.) NOT ordinal numbers (1st, 2nd, 3rd).
  4. EXCEPTION: Use the ordinal form when referring to the day alone, i.e. without the month (e.g. 'on the 25th').
  5. Use the 24-hour-system for time, e.g. 01:00, 14:00, 23:00, etc. (NO am or pm).
  6. In the Sleep graphs, never place a 0 before single-digit hours or minutes, e.g. 2h 30m NOT 02h 30m.
  7. PLEASE NOTE: Down the line the time-and-date format will be more locale-specific.
  8. PLEASE NOTE: User/system/locale preferences may override these guidelines.

Terminology & spelling preferences

  1. LifeQ-powered NOT LifeQ-enabled (note the hyphen, please)
  2. Tracker NOT device
  3. Connect tracker NOT link tracker
  4. Profile NOT LifeQ profile or user profile or LifeQ ID (except when referring to the actual LifeQ ID number given in the user’s profile) Report NOT LifeQ Report
  5. "profile" and "report" must be written with lowercase letters, unless referring to the Profile screen/tab or Report screen/tab.
  6. Button NOT device key (tracker)
  7. Sign in/out NOT log in/out
  8. Sign in to NOT sign into
  9. Remove tracker NOT deregister tracker
  10. Upload data NOT sync tracker/device
  11. In LQUP/LQEA NOT on
  12. Get help - Redirects to a help page with information on how to solve their problem.
  13. Contact Support - Redirects to a page with direct contact details, or it opens up an email pre-populated with Support's email address.
  14. Write Support with uppercase S when referring to Support team.
  15. Username NOT user name
  16. VO2 submax NOT sub-max or sub max
  17. Drop-down menu NOT dropdown or drop down
  18. Graphs NOT charts
  19. Verify email/profile NOT validate
  20. Well-being NOT wellbeing (US preference)
  21. OTA FW = remote software update
  22. Asset snippets and pages = feature information
  23. Latest version NOT latest release
  24. Help Site NOT Help site or help site
  25. On tracker: Swipe NOT scroll
  26. Oriented NOT orientated
  27. Tap vs. click: You tap links on touch screens; you click links with a mouse or track pad

Spacing

  1. There must always be a space between a numeral and a measuring unit, so 1 km NOT 1km; 50 kg NOT 50kg, etc.

Numerals

  1. Always use numerals instead of writing out a number, e.g. 2 not two.
  2. EXCEPTION TO ABOVE: In longer copy, e.g. the Help Site, asset pages, emails, etc. write out zero to nine, and use numerals from 10 onwards.
  3. Also write out numerals at the start of sentences.
  4. Break the above two rules if there are two numbers next to each other, e.g. four 20-year-olds or two 4 km-runs.
  5. Separate decimals using full stops, e.g. 1.5. DO NOT USE A COMMA.
  6. Use a space to separate thousands, e.g. 1 500. DO NOT USE A COMMA.
  7. Give only 1 decimal point for weight values, e.g. 120.4 lbs or 60.8 kg.
  8. There must be no space between a value and percentage symbol (%), so 50% NOT 50 %.

Punctuation

  1. No full stops in headings, buttons or in-line error messages (there may be exceptions to this rule - use your discretion).
  2. Use full stops after all other full sentences.
  3. No punctuation in email greetings, e.g. Hi John NOT Hi, John, AND NOT Hi John,
  4. Exclamation points are acceptable, but must not be overused.
  5. If the user must complete an action below the sentence, add a colon after the instruction. E.g. Please choose a measuring system: <METRIC/IMPERIAL>
  6. Make correct use of hyphens. Pay special attention to compound adjectives, e.g. in-depth study, cutting-edge
  7. technology, state-of-the-art equipment, 20-year-old woman.
  8. Mind apostrophes! They must only be used to indicate possession, NOT plurality. So “Suzanne's tracker” NOT “Suzanne has many tracker's”.
  9. There is only one exception to the above rule: when
  10. referring to multiple lowercase letters, e.g. s's, e's, x's.
  11. Always use double quotation marks. So “ NOT ‘.
  12. No spaces in or around an ellipsis (...).
  13. To insert an ellipsis, always use the ellipsis symbol NOT three full stops.
  14. After a colon: Always start with a lowercase letter, EXCEPT when the colon is followed by a subtitle or preceded by a title or short introductory phrase (e.g. WARNING or "Please note"), in which case the first letter after the colon must be uppercase.
  15. Punctuation with brackets: When text within brackets forms part of another sentence, punctuation must be placed outside the brackets. E.g. "Time asleep includes light, deep and REM sleep (but not time awake)."
  16. Punctuation with brackets: When text within brackets is separate from the text surrounding it, the punctuation must be placed inside the brackets. E.g. "Time asleep includes light, deep and REM sleep. (Please note that time asleep does not include time awake.)"
  17. Bulleted lists: No hard-and-fast rule. Make a call for each separate project and be consistent. E.g.:
    • On the Help Site, all bullets start with a capital letter and end with a full stop.

Uppercase vs. lowercase

App:

  1. All headings in sign-up, profile management and tracker management must be sentence case (i.e. capitalize only the first word and any proper nouns).
  2. All headings in the report and asset snippets and pages must be title case (i.e. capitalize the first letter of each word).
  3. All round buttons must be sentence case. EXCEPTION: Android buttons can be all caps.
  4. All corner buttons must be all caps.
  5. Asset headers (SLEEP, HEART RATE, etc.) in report: all caps.
  6. Body copy (including mentions of assets) must be sentence case.
  7. Brand names must be capitalized, but not what they are referring to, e.g. Striiv tracker NOT Striiv Tracker.
  8. "profile" and "report" must be written with lowercase letters, unless referring to the Profile screen/tab or Report screen/tab.
  9. All other screen, tab and graph names (e.g. Sleep screen, Heart Rate screen, etc.) must also be capitalized.

Other:

  1. Help Site: See additional guidelines.
  2. Email campaigns and other recruitment elements: Use sentence case for headings.

Subscripts and superscripts

  1. Always subscript the 2 in VO2.
  2. Always superscript ©, ™ and ®.
  3. Always superscript asterisks (*) and reference numbers and symbols.
  4. Reference numbers and symbols must come after any punctuation, except en/em dashes.

Registered trademarks

  1. In the app, Striiv must always be followed by the ® symbol (with no space between "Striiv" and the symbol).
  2. Remember to superscript the symbol.
  3. Other trackers and devices: Always find out from the manufacturer whether a trademark symbol is necessary.

Abbreviations

  1. At the first mention of a term on a screen or in an asset page: Write out the term and place the abbreviation in brackets afterwards.
  2. In subsequent mentions in paragraphs: Always write out the term.
  3. Use of abbreviations are permitted in the report.
  4. In the app, both days and months are abbreviated to three letters with NO punctuation (e.g. Mon, Tue, Wed, etc., and Jan, Feb, Mar, etc.).
  5. In other written elements, always write out the days of the week and months of the year.
  6. Measuring units (km, kg, etc.) must always be abbreviated.

Referencing LifeQ in writing

  1. Always capitalize the “L” of LifeQ.
  2. Always write out the name LifeQ - do NOT use LQ, lifeQ or Lifeq.
  3. Do not use the possessive form of LifeQ.
    • DON’T: Part of LifeQ's ecosystem
    • DO: Part of the LifeQ ecosystem
