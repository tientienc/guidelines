Iconography

LifeQ icons are a cohesive family that follows a few rules to make sure they look related. Their definitive feature is that they consist of one single color and are made up of lines only.

There are two types of icons that can be used:

Product icons

  • Used to replace words.
  • Used to add visual elements.
  • Used to create breaths between words and separation between main points, paragraphs, and bullet points.
  • Used to aid you visually for faster comprehension.

System icons

  • Used by app team ONLY. This means DO NOT use system icons for anything other than APP purposes.
  • Used for menu bars, nav bars, and other forms of navigation/menu items.
  • System icons are never larger than 24 px.

Icon do's and don'ts

Do NOT squish or stretch an icon.

Do NOT resize icon to the point where the width of the icon is thinner than the fonts being used or thicker than the headers being used.

Multiple sizes are provided - please use accordingly.

Icon attributes

Stroke attribute

  • 1 px stroke width
  • Rounded cap (edge)
  • Rounded corners
  • Stroke align center

Icons MUST contain a gap or line accent
(within icon)

There must be at least 1 PX BOX of distance between lines. If space is being added between lines or sections of an icon, it cannot be a random distance (try to use multiples). All LifeQ icons contain either a small gap, a line accent, or significant negative space.

NO crossing lines

If icon is in front of something (e.g. a person in front of a building), erase extra noise within icon for simplification (i.e. building should not be visible within person's head).

NO building of icons or erasing of padding

Icons may NOT be placed on top of each other and they may NOT be put together to build a bigger icon or image. The padding is part of the full icon and should be respected.

In Adobe Illustrator, DO NOT ever have the scale corner and stroke checked. This can be checked in the Transform panel in Illustrator.

Grid system

This is the grid system that all the icons are built from.

  • It is built in multiples of nine.
  • It has four shapes that every icon should somehow adhere to, whether it be the landscape rectangle, portrait rectangle, big circle, square or small circle.

Duos

Duos are slightly different to icons. They may also only consist of lines, but the key difference is they consist of two colors. You also have more freedom in terms of their sizing and how they can be used.

Duos follow the same attribute guidelines as icons. Please review the icon attributes to properly apply them to duos.

Usage

Duos serve as more than a replacement of a word. They can serve as a page feature, description of a section, or a how-to.

Examples include:

  • Feature display
  • Header image
  • Banner image
  • Instruction purposes

Exceptions do exist.

Parameters

  • Unlike icons, there are no maximum or set sizes duos must follow.
  • The smallest size a duo can be is 24 px.

Grid system

Like icons, the grid system is based on multiples of nine. As for sizing, duos are not constrained to the 24, 48, 88, and 108 proportions.

Color system

  • A duo consists of a base color and an accent color. The base color may only consist of the primary colors. As for accent colors, primary or secondary colors may be used.
  • When naming colors, the first color name is the base color and the second color name is the accent color (see "Naming duos" below for more information).

Naming duos

The duos are represented as “duo”

Naming a duo follows the same naming formula as icons.

Duo naming format

DUO_NAME_COLOR_SIZE.FORMAT

duo_timer_DB-EM_48px
duo_connecting-tracker_BL-EM_108px

duo_timer_DB_EM_108px
duo-timer-48px

Duo do's and don'ts

  • Do NOT cross duos and lines.
  • Do NOT marry icons and duos.
  • Duos may not be placed on top of other icons as a cluster.
  • DO NOT break the padding barrier.

Images

Images are a bit of their own entity. They are unique because they can consist of a variety of colors and include attributes such as shading and shadowing. There are no grids or restrictions for the size of an image. However, they follow the consistency of the line design and spacing that is found in icons and duos.

Usage

  • Used to help visualize a bigger concept or story.
  • Used for data visualization.
  • Used for feature images.
  • Used for infographics.
  • Used for merchandise.
  • Used for commercial purposes.

Image attributes

  • 1 px stroke width
  • Rounded cap (edge)
  • Rounded corners
  • Stroke align center
  • Features: shading or shadows

Color system

  • Use LQDB as the primary color.
  • Use LifeQ Gray, LifeQ Background 1, LifeQ Background 2, or any LifeQ Faded color for shadows.
  • Use LifeQ Blue and a LifeQ secondary color for shading.
  • No colors need to be included when naming an image.

Naming images

The images are represented as “img”

Naming an image follows the same naming conventions as icons, except color is not included.

Image naming format

IMG-TYPE_NAME.FORMAT

img_sleep
infographic_sleep-metric
graph_life-expectancy

img-activity-suite-cover-image
img_ActivitySuite_CoverImage

Types of images
